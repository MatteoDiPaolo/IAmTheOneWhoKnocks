WHAT

  • A door that should always be open

Don't you got just five minutes to talk?

  • Scheduled time in order to talk about you
  • Getting the most out of it
    • Big responsability
    • Listening
    • Caring
    • Not being missed
  • 5 min to talk --> stress, anxiety, etc.
  • If you do it properly that door will stay open forever (Felipe, Laura, Dani)
  • It is a big responsibility, they must know that you invest time on it
  • Bad managers are missing, do not care, do not listen (really listen)

WHY

  1. Unblocking
  2. Dealing with problems and conflicts
  3. Give and get feedback
  4. Career development
  5. Measuring temperature
  6. Relationship development
  7. Alignment checking
  • Unblocking (prep a meeting with a stakeholder, prep a presentation, etc.)
  • Their problems are your problems, solve them or scale the up and check on their resolution
  • Correct certain behaviours
  • Coaching, mentoring, perfomance and goals followup, set expectations, steer and correct
  • Keep motivation up, detect and prevent bornouts
  • Getting to know strengthness, weakness to detect up and down moments
  • Being aligned is the most important bit

TIPS

“ You see, you start out with a little bit of oil. Then you fry some garlic. Then you throw in some tomatoes, tomato paste, you fry it; ya make sure it doesn’t stick. You get it to a boil; you shove in all your sausage and your meatballs; heh…? And a little bit o’ wine. An’ a little bit o’ sugar, and that’s my trick. ”
  • Half an hour every two weeks
  • Be genuine and keep your word
  • Take notes
  • Get in the flow and make it feel natural
  • Do not miss them, it will look like it does not matter
  • Problems must be solved and escalated and yuo cannot make them slip
  • Notes help to keep track of things, actions, they avoid misunderstandings
  • Make it awkward, do not hesitate to ask directly, get to the nasty bits if needed

FIRST ONE ON ONE

  1. Why are we here?
  2. What do you do in your free time? who are you? Tell me more about yourself
  3. How would you like to receive feedback?
  4. What do you expect from a manager?
  5. How do you feel about your role?
  6. Where does your motivation come from?
  • Explain them why we are doing this
  • Get to know the person, their life, problems, way of living
  • Mentor? Unblocker? etc.
  • Which is your target role?
  • Inner passion? Just a medium?
  • Tell them about yourself as well
    • I am always available, do not wait for the one on ones
    • I delegate a lot challenging people with new tasks
    • I expect them to define their own goals
  • They must leave with a feeling that they will be heard and valued

SUBSEQUENT ONE ON ONES

  1. How are you doing?
    • In the project
    • In the company
    • In your life
  2. What can I do for you?
    • Unblocking issues
  3. Goals followup
  4. Give and receive feedback

FEEDBACK

  • The good one always
    • Everytime
  • The bad one is just a improvement opportunity
    • Is it really needed to be shared?
  • The ugly one
    • It has to be shared

DISCUSS

  1. Are they acting like the best manager / report / partner you could wish for?
    • Are you?
    • Why or why not?
    • Discuss
  2. What have you already told someone else about this person (or heard others say)?
    • Share with the
    • Discuss
  3. Dont be a toxic manager
  • I was a toxic manager - With Laura and David
  • I was a good pushing manager - With Daniel

BIO method (objectivity)

  1. Behavior
    • Observe a specific behavior and then explain what you observed
  2. Impact
    • Tell them what kind of impact that behavior had on you, the team, and / or the organization
  3. Option
    • Provide a suggestion or an expectation for future behavior, or alternatively ask how the other person wants to move forward
